Shared Mobility effects on Bus Ridership

Visualization of factors effecting ridership of the MUNI bus system in San Francisco, California.

San Francisco has experienced employment growth over the past decade, but the MUNI bus ridership has not followed this trend. This project aims to shed light on the factors that could be causing the flatline in MUNI ridership. Python is used to efficiently visualize spatially all of the potential factors. Data Trends in Mode Choice for San Francisco and other U.S. Cities: A Comparative Analysis

A Study done by John and Gina.

I am John Martin. I am from Georgetown, Kentucky, and am a senior at the University of Kentucky graduating in May 2017 with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Civil Engineering. Some of my hobbies include riding my bike and playing basketball and Ping-Pong.

Currently the city of San Francisco Municipal Transit Authority (SFMTA) is producing a Travel Decision Survey each year for public consumption. These reports have data on people’s preferred modes of transportation around the bay area. The goal of our project is to find trends in mode choices from year to year in San Francisco as well as in other selected cities around the United States. This data will then be compared to trends in national mode choice data, which will come from American Fact Finder.
